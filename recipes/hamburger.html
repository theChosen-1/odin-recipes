Hamburger:

Description:

A simple cheese hamburger is a timeless classic loved for its satisfying, no-fuss combination of flavors. At its core, it features a juicy beef patty grilled or pan-fried to perfection, topped with a slice of melted cheese—usually cheddar—tucked between a soft, lightly toasted burger bun. The heat from the patty melts the cheese just enough to create a creamy, savory layer that blends beautifully with the meat.

This humble burger is often dressed with classic toppings like crisp lettuce, sliced tomato, onions, pickles, ketchup, and mustard, though it can be enjoyed plain as well. It’s the perfect balance of savory, salty, and slightly tangy elements in every bite—ideal for a quick meal, backyard barbecue, or diner-style comfort food experience.

Ingridients:

  • Ground beef (for patties)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Cheese slices (e.g., cheddar or American)
  • Burger buns
  • Lettuce (optional)
  • Tomato slices (optional)
  • Onion slices (optional)
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Ketchup (optional)
  • Mustard (optional)
  • Butter or oil (for toasting buns)

Steps:

  1. Form Patties - Season ground beef with salt and pepper, then shape into flat, round patties.
  2. Cook Patties - Grill or pan-fry the patties over medium-high heat for about 3–4 minutes per side, or until fully cooked.
  3. Add Cheese - Place a slice of cheese on each patty during the last minute of cooking to let it melt.
  4. Toast Buns - Lightly butter the burger buns and toast them on a pan or grill until golden.
  5. Assemble Burger - Place the cooked patty with melted cheese on the bottom bun, then add lettuce, tomato, onions, pickles, and desired sauces.
  6. Serve - Top with the other half of the bun and serve hot.
